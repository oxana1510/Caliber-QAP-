Did you know

National Master Specification (NMS) section for SprayFoam has been updated?

Did you know that the Canadian National Master Construction Specification (NMS) update in January 2019 included technical changes to Sprayed Insulation – Polyurethane Foam (Section 07 21 29)

The National Research Council of Canada conducted consultations with spray foam industry technical experts over the past several years to update this section of the NMS. Those efforts have resulted in a newer and better specification for spray foam.

  • 1

    Reference to the Canadian Urethane Foam Contractors Association (CUFCA) has been removed as there are currently four (4) organizations in Canada recognized by the Canadian Construction Material Centre (CCMC) as third-party quality assurance bodies for spray foam products.

  • 2

    Installers must be certified by a personnel certification body accredited to ISO 17024.

You can download the latest version from the National Research Council Canada website.

QUALITY ASSURANCE PROGRAM 101

The Quality Assurance Program (QAP) manages risks that may occur during the installation of spray foam. The QAP provides a clear framework to deliver assurance that spray foam meet defined quality expectations and is installed in conformance with applicable standards and manufacturer requirements. Fundamentally, the QAP is in place to provide confidence regarding the quality of installation in the field. Done right the first time and everytime is our goal. The QAP encompasses a variety of actions that include training and education, objective qualification of contractors and installers, a tool for installers QA/QC self check, a documentation process to create records, access to an independent auditor to conduct compliance audits , and a process to deal with the problem installations.
